Jaeeon Lee

I am a PhD student in Bernardo Sabatini lab at the Harvard Medical School.

I joined the Ph.D program in Neurobiology in 2015, and joined the Sabatini lab in 2016. Before, I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Bio-engineering at KAIST in 2015. As an undergrad, I previously worked in Gerstner's lab, Wickens' lab, Medina lab and Jung's lab.

I am broadly interested in circuit mechanisms of computations relevant for decision making. I used a combination of anatomical tracing, optogenetics, in vivo extracellular recording, and computational modelling.

Research

Work during my Ph.D in Sabatini lab

Striatal D2 pathway mediate competition during lateralized action selection



Jaeeon Lee

, Bernardo Sabatini
manuscript in prep.

We dissected the functional impact of stimulating the striatal indirect pathway, on behavior and circuit downstream (colliculus). We show push-pull modulation of SC dynamics, explaining paradoxical behavioral effect of stimuation. Dimensionality reduction of activity reveals modulation specifically along the choice direction.

Anatomically segregated basal ganglia pathways allow parallel behavioral modulation



Jaeeon Lee

, Wengang Wang, Bernardo Sabatini
Nature Neuroscience (accepted), 2020

We show topographical loops starting from striatum to Pf, VM and SC in the mouse brain. Segregated pathways in medial vs ventrolateral striatum modulate turning/licking behavior respectively.

Depth-resolved fiber photometry with a single tapered optical fiber implant

Filippo Pisano, Marco Pisanello, Suk Joon Lee,

Jaeeon Lee

, et al
Nature Methods, 2019

We show the application of tapered fibers in depth-dependent photometry. We simultaenously recorded dopamine signals in dorsal/ventral striatum in the same mouse and show signals can diverge at specficic behavioral events.
